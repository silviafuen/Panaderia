Our Kitchen

At an early age, Master Chef Jose was in a Mom-and-Pop Bakery cleaning pots and pans where he began to learn how to make traditional pan dulce. With his enthusiasm and passion for baking bread he's kept our traditions alive between the walls of La Tapatia café with bakery items such as Pan de Muerto, Buñuelos, Roscas de Reyes, and many more.

Our Story

In 1941, Pete and Cruz Cortez opened a little three-table cafe for early-rising farmers and workers at Fort Worth’s Mercado. 75 years later La Tapatia is a world-famous landmark.
